    Particle Model: Expansion, Contraction and Density

    Key Concepts

    Diffusion

    Definition: Gases or liquids mix without anything moving them because the particles are moving around all the time.

    • Particles move randomly
    • No external force required
    • Occurs in gases and liquids
    • Driven by kinetic energy of particles

    Brownian Motion

    Definition: Tiny bits of dust in air or water can be seen jiggling around as they are hit by the moving air or water particles.

    • Evidence of particle movement
    • Visible under microscope
    • Caused by collisions with invisible particles
    • Supports kinetic theory

    Expanding and Contracting

    Heating: - Materials expand when heated - Particles move faster - Particles take up more space - Increased kinetic energy

    Cooling: - Materials contract when cooled - Particles move slower - Particles take up less space - Decreased kinetic energy

    Density Changes

    Formula: Density = Mass / Volume

    When material contracts: - Same mass in smaller volume - Density increases

    When material expands: - Same mass in larger volume - Density decreases

    Real-World Applications

    1. Thermometers (Photo E)

    How they work: - Liquid expands when heated - Liquid rises up the tube - Expansion provides temperature reading - Reservoir contains liquid

    Components: - Reservoir of liquid (mercury or alcohol) - Narrow tube - Temperature scale

    2. Train Tracks (Photo D)

    Problem: - Train tracks can buckle and twist in very hot weather - Caused by thermal expansion

    Solution: - Gaps left between rail sections - Allows for expansion - Prevents buckling

    3. Bridges (Photo F)

    Expansion Joints: - Buildings, bridges and structures change size all the time - Built with gaps (expansion joints) - Materials expand without bending or breaking - Prevents structural damage

    Particle Behavior When Heated

    Solids

    Key Points: - Particles vibrate further about fixed positions - Particles themselves do not change size - Increased vibration = more space needed

    Review Questions

    Question 5

    A substance cools down when energy is transferred away from it. Explain how this affects:

    a) The movement of the particles
    b) The size of the object

    Answer Guide: - Particles move slower - Object contracts (gets smaller)

    Question 6

    Look at Photo E (thermometer):

    a) Explain how a thermometer works
    b) Suggest how you could make your own thermometer

    Answer Guide: - Liquid expands when heated - Rises in narrow tube - DIY: Bottle, colored water, straw, seal

    Question 7

    A bridge is built without expansion gaps. Explain what could happen if the temperature became:

    a) Much hotter than the day it was built
    b) Much colder than the day it was built

    Answer Guide: - Hot: Bridge expands, could buckle or crack - Cold: Bridge contracts, could create stress fractures

    By the end of this topic, you should be able to:

    • [ ] Describe the properties of different states of matter
    • [ ] Explain the properties in terms of the particle model
    • [ ] Explain why materials expand and contract when temperature changes
    • [ ] Understand real-world applications of thermal expansion
    • [ ] Calculate and explain density changes

    Key Terms

    Term Definition
    Diffusion Mixing of gases/liquids due to particle movement
    Brownian Motion Random movement of particles suspended in fluid
    Expansion Increase in volume when heated
    Contraction Decrease in volume when cooled
    Density Mass per unit volume
    Expansion Joint Gap built into structures to allow for expansion

    Did You Know?

    FACT: Train tracks can buckle and twist in very hot weather if expansion gaps are not properly maintained!

    Study Tips

    1. Remember: Particles themselves don't change size - only the space between them changes
    2. Visualize: Draw particle diagrams for hot vs cold materials
    3. Connect: Link theory to real examples (thermometers, bridges, train tracks)
    4. Practice: Explain concepts in your own words
