# 7Ib: Energy Transfers and Stores

## Learning Objectives
- Understand how energy is stored and transferred
- Identify different forms of energy storage
- Explain the law of conservation of energy

---

## Key Concepts

### What is Energy Storage?
- **Energy can be stored** in various forms
- **Chemical energy**: Energy stored in chemical substances
  - Food
  - Petrol
  - Cells (batteries)

### Energy Transfer
**Definition**: When energy moves from one store to another, it is **transferred**.

**Energy can be transferred by:**
- Heating
- Light
- Sound
- Electricity
- Forces

### Kinetic Energy
- Moving objects store energy
- This is called **kinetic energy**

### Law of Conservation of Energy
> **Energy cannot be created or destroyed**; it can only be transferred and stored in different ways.

---

## Real-World Examples

### Fireworks (Photo A)
- Chemical substances inside firework rockets **store energy**
- When the firework goes off, energy is transferred by:
  - Light
  - Sound
  - Heating

### Fairground Rides (Photo B)
- Powered by diesel generators
- Energy stored in diesel fuel (chemical substances)
- Energy transferred by:
  - Electricity
  - Forces
- Results in kinetic energy (moving people and rides)

## Questions and Answers

### Question 1
**Which of the rides shown in photo A needs the most energy and which the least energy? Explain your answers.**

**Answer:**
- **Most energy**: Large roller coasters or drops - they need to lift heavy cars with multiple passengers to great heights and move them at high speeds
- **Least energy**: Smaller rides like carousels - they move slowly and don't need to overcome as much gravity or friction
