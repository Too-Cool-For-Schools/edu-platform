    7Ib: Energy Transfers and Stores

    Learning Objectives

    • Understand how energy is stored and transferred
    • Identify different forms of energy storage
    • Explain the law of conservation of energy

    Key Concepts

    What is Energy Storage?

    • Energy can be stored in various forms
    • Chemical energy: Energy stored in chemical substances
    • Food
    • Petrol
    • Cells (batteries)

    Energy Transfer

    Definition: When energy moves from one store to another, it is transferred.

    Energy can be transferred by: - Heating - Light - Sound - Electricity - Forces

    Kinetic Energy

    • Moving objects store energy
    • This is called kinetic energy

    Law of Conservation of Energy

    Energy cannot be created or destroyed; it can only be transferred and stored in different ways.

    Real-World Examples

    Fireworks (Photo A)

    • Chemical substances inside firework rockets store energy
    • When the firework goes off, energy is transferred by:
    • Light
    • Sound
    • Heating

    Fairground Rides (Photo B)

    • Powered by diesel generators
    • Energy stored in diesel fuel (chemical substances)
    • Energy transferred by:
    • Electricity
    • Forces
    • Results in kinetic energy (moving people and rides)

    Questions and Answers

    Question 1

    Which of the rides shown in photo A needs the most energy and which the least energy? Explain your answers.

    Answer: - Most energy: Large roller coasters or drops - they need to lift heavy cars with multiple passengers to great heights and move them at high speeds - Least energy: Smaller rides like carousels - they move slowly and don't need to overcome as much gravity or friction

    Question 2

    2a) Five different things happening in photos A and B that need energy:

    1. Fireworks exploding (light and sound)
    2. Fairground ride moving
    3. People walking around the park
    4. Diesel generator running
    5. Lights illuminating the rides

    2b) How is energy provided for these things?

    1. Fireworks: Chemical energy stored in explosives
    2. Rides: Diesel fuel → electricity and forces
    3. People: Chemical energy from food
    4. Generator: Chemical energy in diesel fuel
    5. Lights: Electrical energy from generators

    Question 3

    3a) Five things done yesterday that needed energy:

    1. Walking to school
    2. Eating breakfast
    3. Using a phone/computer
    4. Riding a bus/bicycle
    5. Studying/reading

    3b) Most and least energy:

    • Most energy: Walking/cycling - required physical movement using muscle power
    • Least energy: Reading - mainly mental activity with minimal physical movement
    • How I know: Physical activities make you tired and burn more calories than sedentary activities

    Question 1 (Page 138)

    1a) Three things that transfer energy by heating or light:

    1. Sun (light and heating)
    2. Electric heater (heating)
    3. Light bulb (light)

    1b) Three things that transfer energy by sound:

    1. Speakers
    2. Musical instruments
    3. Fireworks

    1c) Three things that use energy transferred by electricity:

    1. Television
    2. Refrigerator
    3. Computer

    Question 2 (Page 138)

    Diesel fuel is a store of energy in chemicals. Write down three other things that store energy in chemicals.

    Answer: 1. Food - stores chemical energy for our bodies 2. Petrol - stores chemical energy for vehicles 3. Batteries - store chemical energy for electrical devices

    Summary

    ✓ Energy is stored in food, fuels, and batteries as chemical energy
