    How Do Our Bodies Use Energy?

    Introduction

    Humans and other animals need energy to live. We use energy for:

    • Growth – building new cells and tissues
    • Repair – healing wounds, replacing worn-out cells
    • Movement – walking, running, breathing, even blinking
    • Keeping warm – maintaining body temperature (especially in cold environments)

    Our bodies get this energy from food.

    Measuring Energy in Food

    Units of Energy

    Unit Symbol Equivalent
    Joule J Base unit of energy
    Kilojoule kJ 1 kJ = 1000 J

    Example: The energy needed to lift an apple from the floor onto a table is about 1 J. Most foods contain much more — so we use kilojoules (kJ).

    Reading Nutrition Labels

    Nutrition labels show how much energy (and nutrients) are stored in food. They usually give values:

    • Per serving (e.g., per bun, per burger)
    • Per 100g (for easy comparison between products)

    Example Label: Falafel Burger & Bun

    🟩 Bun (65g)

    Nutrient Per bun (65g) Per 100g
    Energy 544 kJ / 130 kcal 837 kJ / 200 kcal
    Protein 6 g 9.2 g
    Carbohydrate 21 g 32.3 g
    Fat 2.5 g 3.85 g
    Fibre 1.2 g 1.8 g
    Sodium 0.2 g 0.3 g

    🟥 Falafel Burger (125g)

    Nutrient Per burger (125g) Per 100g
    Energy 1990 kJ / 476 kcal 1592 kJ / 381 kcal
    Protein 17 g 13.6 g
    Carbohydrate 63 g 50 g
    Fat 15 g 12 g
    Fibre 7 g 5.6 g
    Sodium 2 g 1.6 g

    ✅ Note: “of which sugar” or “of which saturates” refers to subcategories within carbs/fats.

    Calculation Examples

    🔢 Example 1: Energy in 100g of falafel burger

    From the label:

    Per 100g → Energy = 1592 kJ

    ✅ So, 100g of falafel burger contains 1592 kJ of energy.

    🔢 Example 2: Mark eats two burgers (each = 1 falafel burger + 1 bun). How much total energy?

    Step 1: Find energy per full meal (burger + bun)

    • Falafel burger (125g): 1990 kJ
    • Bun (65g): 544 kJ

    → Total per meal = 1990 + 544 = 2534 kJ

    Step 2: Multiply by 2 meals

    → 2534 × 2 = 5068 kJ

    ✅ Mark consumes 5068 kJ of energy from eating two falafel burgers with buns.

    Why Do Different People Need Different Amounts of Energy?

    Your body’s energy needs depend on:

    • Age – children and teens need more for growth
    • Activity level – exercise burns more energy than watching TV
    • Body size – larger bodies need more energy to maintain
    • Gender – males often need slightly more due to muscle mass
    • Health status – illness or recovery may increase needs

    If you do a lot of exercise, you need more energy than if you spend most of your time watching television.

    Review Questions

    Question 1: Why does your body need food?

    Answer:
    Food provides energy for living processes: growing, repairing tissues, moving, and staying warm. It also supplies nutrients like protein, vitamins, and minerals essential for health.

    Question 2a: How much energy does 100g of falafel burger contain? Give your answer in kilojoules.

    Answer:
    From the nutrition label:
    → 1592 kJ

    Question 2b: Mark eats two burgers (each burger is one falafel burger in a bun). How much energy is in the food he eats?

    Answer:
    Energy per burger + bun = 1990 kJ + 544 kJ = 2534 kJ
    For two meals: 2534 × 2 = 5068 kJ

    Final Answer: 5068 kJ

    Key Takeaways

    ✔️ Energy is measured in joules (J) and kilojoules (kJ); 1 kJ = 1000 J
    ✔️ Food labels show energy content per serving and per 100g
    ✔️ Your body uses energy for growth, repair, movement, and warmth
    ✔️ Active people need more energy than sedentary people
    ✔️ Always check both components when calculating total energy (e.g., burger + bun!)

    Study Tip

    Practice reading real food labels at home! Compare snacks, drinks, or meals — calculate total energy for different portions. This helps build real-world math and science skills.
